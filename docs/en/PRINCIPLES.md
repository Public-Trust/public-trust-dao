# PRINCIPLES AND PROHIBITIONS — PUBLIC TRUST DAO

> Normative document. Derived from [`CONSTITUTION.md`](CONSTITUTION.md) and
> [`MANIFESTO.md`](MANIFESTO.md). The prohibitions below are binding and must be
> observed literally in code, in AI agents, in governance, and in any public text.
>
> Unfamiliar technical words from this document are explained in plain language in the
> [glossary](GLOSSARY.md) — the "Basics" group.

## Foundational principles

- human dignity
- compassion
- mutual aid
- transparency
- voluntariness
- absence of exploitation
- absence of sole power
- public benefit
- open source
- protection against abuse
- justice
- long-term sustainability
- development of self-reliance
- priority of real benefit

## Principles of the blockchain architecture

openness · transparency · immutability of records · absence of a central
owner · distributed governance · verifiability · open source ·
interoperability with other protocols.

## Constitutional prohibitions (literal)

It is prohibited:

1. to promise returns;
2. to guarantee profit;
3. to create financial pyramids;
4. to pay funds for recruiting new people;
5. to concentrate power;
6. to conceal operations;
7. to use the fund for personal gain;
8. corruption;
9. favoritism;
10. discrimination.

## Constitution of human dignity

Every person possesses dignity regardless of: income; origin; education; age;
nationality; state of health; social standing.

Aid must increase a person's self-reliance, not their dependency.

## Application in code and agents

- Smart contracts must not contain functions that give a single address sole
  control over funds (violation of prohibition No. 5).
- No reward function may depend on the number of invited
  addresses/participants (prohibition No. 4, article 6.2 of the constitution).
- No public text (website, README, interfaces) may promise income,
  profit, or guaranteed gain (prohibitions No. 1, No. 2).
- All treasury operations must be publicly readable (prohibition No. 6).
