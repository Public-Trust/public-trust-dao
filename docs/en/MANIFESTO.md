# PUBLIC TRUST DAO v1.0
## Unified manifesto, constitution, goals, blockchain architecture, and technical specification

---

## Mission
To build an open, decentralized system of mutual support and public goods that provides people with basic safety, human dignity, housing, food, medical care, education, the restoration of self-reliance, and the ability to help other people and society.

The project is not an investment scheme, a financial pyramid, or a tool for extracting profit.

The project is an open mutual-aid infrastructure governed by a constitution, a transparent blockchain treasury, open code, artificial intelligence, and the community.

## Core principle
No one owns the project. No one is a permanent beneficiary. The founder is an ordinary participant. The project belongs to its mission, its constitution, and the community. People and AI are temporary stewards of this mission.

## Blockchain foundation of the project
Public Trust DAO is a blockchain-native public-goods system, built on public blockchain networks and open protocols. In the initial phase, no proprietary blockchain is created. Existing networks are used: Polygon; Ethereum; other open networks as needed.

## Principles of the blockchain architecture
openness; transparency; immutability of records; absence of a central owner; distributed governance; verifiability; open code; compatibility with other protocols.

## Highest goal
The creation of a sustainable global network of mutual aid, in which a person who has received support eventually gains the ability to help other people.

## Foundational principles
human dignity; compassion; mutual aid; transparency; voluntariness; absence of exploitation; absence of sole power; public benefit; open code; protection against abuse; fairness; long-term sustainability; the development of self-reliance; the priority of real benefit.

## Main areas of activity

**Housing:** prevention of homelessness; help with rent; help with utility payments; temporary housing; emergency placement; help with relocation; cooperative housing; public housing stock; construction of public housing.

**Food:** food assistance; hot meals; grocery delivery; support for children; support for families; support for elderly people.

**Health:** treatment; medicines; dentistry; psychological support; diagnostics; rehabilitation; help for people with disabilities; palliative care; emergency medical support.

**Support for families:** help for children; help for pregnant women; help for single parents; help for large families; help in the event of the loss of a breadwinner.

**Support for elderly people:** care; companionship; grocery delivery; household assistance; help paying for essential expenses.

**Education:** retraining; vocational training; languages; digital literacy; open knowledge; support for talented people.

**Employment:** help finding work; help with documents; support for self-employment; acquisition of tools; creation of jobs; support for cooperatives.

**Emergency aid:** loss of housing; loss of work; humanitarian crises; natural disasters; emergencies.

**Animal support:** treatment of animals; feeding of animals; help for shelters; animal rescue.

**Social integration:** help for lonely people; help for migrants; restoration of social ties; help for people after difficult life situations.

**Public goods:** open-source software; scientific projects; educational projects; environmental projects; digital infrastructure; free access to knowledge.

**Support for fund participants:** organizers, founders, and participants may receive help on equal terms with other people.

## Reward for socially useful activity
help for elderly people; help for children; grocery delivery; home repairs; volunteering; teaching; project development; programming; legal assistance; medical assistance; community moderation; help for animals; accompanying those in need; creation of educational materials.

**Reward never depends on recruiting new participants.**

## Constitutional prohibitions
It is prohibited to: promise returns; guarantee profit; create financial pyramids; pay funds for recruiting new people; concentrate power; conceal operations; use the fund for personal gain; corruption; favoritism; discrimination.

## Roles
Person in need; Participant; Volunteer; Trusted participant; Steward; Observer; AI Guardian.

## Reputation system
Taken into account: time of participation; completed tasks; help for other people; absence of violations; community recommendations; duration of participation.

## Anti-abuse
phased payments; limits; collective verification; public reports; audit; appeals; reputational sanctions; temporary restrictions; exclusion of participants.

The system must make honesty more advantageous than fraud.

## Constitution of human dignity
Every person possesses dignity regardless of: income; origin; education; age; nationality; state of health; social standing.

Help must increase a person's self-reliance, not their dependence.

## Constitution of fair distribution (priority)
1. Threat to life.
2. Threat of losing housing.
3. Medical care.
4. Food.
5. Children.
6. Elderly people.
7. Disability.
8. Restoration of self-reliance.
9. Education.
10. Long-term public benefit.

## Public treasury
The fund's resources exist on the blockchain. Used: Safe multisig; smart contracts; DAO modules; public registries.

No single person holds sole control. Starting configuration: 5 stewards; 3 signatures out of 5. The founder has the same rights as the other stewards.

## Bootstrap period
A temporary bootstrap wallet is used only to launch the infrastructure. The bootstrap steward is not considered the owner of the fund. After the community is formed, the funds are transferred to the public treasury.

## Smart contracts
Smart contracts are the digital embodiment of the fund's constitution. They are responsible for: storing funds; voting; treasury management; recording decisions; distributing rewards; reputation; audit; protection against abuse.

## AI agents
Guardian AI; Audit AI; Fairness AI; Reputation AI; Housing AI; Governance AI; Mediator AI; Documentation AI.

AI holds no power. AI owns no funds. AI serves the project's mission and helps uphold the constitution.

## Data storage
GitHub; GitHub Pages; IPFS; distributed storage.

## Technology infrastructure
GitHub; GitHub Pages; Cloudflare Pages; MetaMask; Safe; Polygon; Ethereum; IPFS; Snapshot; Aragon; OpenAI; Claude; local models.

## Publicity
All documents are open. All decisions are explained. All operations are published. All changes are recorded.

## Legal status
In the first phase, the project exists as an Open Source Initiative. Later, the creation of the following is possible: a non-profit foundation; a cooperative; a Public Benefit Foundation; a DAO.

## Ultimate goal
The creation of a global Public Trust DAO — a public digital trust in which resources belong not to individual people, but to the mission, the constitution, and the community.

A person who has received help, where possible, becomes a source of help for others.

The project's core values: human dignity; mutual aid; fairness; public good; society's ability to care for its members.

Neither a person, nor an organization, nor a state, nor artificial intelligence owns the project. The project belongs to its mission, its constitution, and the community.
