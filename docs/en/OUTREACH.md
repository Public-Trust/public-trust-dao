## 0. Outreach rails (read BEFORE any letter)

Outreach is public text on behalf of the project, so it obeys the same
[constitutional prohibitions](PRINCIPLES.md) as all communication. Literally
forbidden:

- ❌ promising the recipient or their audience any yield, profit, "x's", a token
  that will "go up", or any financial gain from participating;
- ❌ calling the project an "investment", a "startup with returns", or an
  "opportunity to earn";
- ❌ paying for a mention, paying to recruit people, offering referral bonuses or
  a "cut" — that would turn outreach into a pyramid;
- ❌ pressuring with urgency/scarcity, begging, exploiting pity, manipulating;
- ❌ asking for money right now (the project is at bootstrap/testnet — there is no
  real treasury or intake of funds yet; we say this honestly);
- ❌ mass-mailing/spam, buying contact lists, sending without consent;
- ❌ publishing or forwarding recipients' personal data.

✅ What we do instead: **honestly describe the mission**, show that everything is
**open and verifiable**, **ask not for money but for attention/advice/co-building**,
respect "no" and silence, write **personally and like a human**, and always leave
a link to the open code and the disclaimer.

> The principle in one line: **we invite people to a shared cause, we don't sell
> an opportunity.** If a letter resembles "invest and earn" in any way, it
> violates the constitution and is not sent.

**Who sends and how.** Only the **operator** sends — from their own mail or the
project's official mail — **fedorgrigorev@proton.me** (the reply address; see
[`EMAIL-SETUP.md`](EMAIL-SETUP.md)) — manually, one by one, personally. The agent
prepares and updates the texts here; the final "go" is a human's.

## 2. How to personalize (so the letter isn't spam)

A template is a skeleton. Before sending, the operator fills in the specifics,
otherwise the letter reads as a mass mailing and is rightly ignored:

1. **Name and what you respect** — one sincere, concrete line (what exactly the
   recipient did), without flattery.
2. **Why them specifically** — link their work to the project's mission (1 sentence).
3. **A clear, modest ask** — ONE concrete action (see "the ladder of asks" below),
   not "support us somehow".
4. **Proof, not words** — a link to the open code/registry/constitution.
5. **Honest status** — bootstrap/testnet, we don't accept money yet.
6. **An easy out** — explicitly "if this isn't relevant, just ignore it, no
   hard feelings."

**The ladder of asks (light to heavy)** — pick ONE, start light:
- ⭐ just learn about the project / give advice or criticism;
- ⭐⭐ share it with those who might find it useful;
- ⭐⭐⭐ connect us with a relevant person/foundation;
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ publicly cover it (a story/mention) once the project is ready;
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ co-participation/partnership (after audit and launch).

We never ask for money "right now" on any rung.

## 4. After a reply (a short protocol for the operator)

- **Positive/neutral reply:** thank them, give exactly what they asked for (a
  link, a short call, a document), don't "push", don't over-promise.
- **Refusal or silence:** respect it. Don't write again (at most one gentle
  follow-up after 2–3 weeks, if a conversation genuinely started). No spam.
- **A request for money / "what do I get":** honestly — the project is at
  bootstrap stage, we don't accept funds, we promise no yield; it's a public good.
- **An interview/story request:** see the "for media" template in
  [`EMAIL-SETUP.md`](EMAIL-SETUP.md) and the facts in [`PROMOTION.md`](PROMOTION.md)
  (the "this is not an investment" FAQ).
- **Recipients' personal data** is not published or forwarded; we don't post
  correspondence without the other party's consent.
