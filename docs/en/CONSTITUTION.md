# CONSTITUTION — PUBLIC TRUST DAO

> The supreme law of the project. All smart contracts, AI agents, governance
> procedures, and public texts must conform to this constitution. In case of any
> conflict between an artifact and the constitution, the constitution prevails.
>
> Full source manifesto: [`MANIFESTO.md`](MANIFESTO.md).
> Related normative documents: [`PRINCIPLES.md`](PRINCIPLES.md),
> [`PRIORITIES.md`](PRIORITIES.md), [`ANTI-ABUSE.md`](ANTI-ABUSE.md).
>
> Unfamiliar technical words from this document are explained in plain language in the
> [glossary](GLOSSARY.md) — the "Basics" group.

---

## Article 1. Nature of the Project

1.1. Public Trust DAO is an open, decentralized system of mutual aid and public
goods. Its purpose is to provide people with basic safety, human dignity,
housing, food, medical care, education, the restoration of self-reliance, and
the opportunity to help others.

1.2. The project **is not** an investment scheme, a financial pyramid, or a
profit-extraction instrument. It is a public good.

1.3. The project is governed by the constitution, a transparent blockchain
treasury, open code, artificial intelligence (as a service tool), and the
community.

## Article 2. No Owner

2.1. No one owns the project. No one is a permanent beneficiary.

2.2. The founder is an ordinary participant and an ordinary guardian — with the
same rights as everyone else.

2.3. The project belongs to its mission, its constitution, and its community.
People and AI are temporary guardians of the mission, not its owners.

2.4. No individual, organization, state, or artificial intelligence may
single-handedly control the project's funds or decisions.

## Article 3. Openness and Verifiability

3.1. All documents are open. All decisions are explained. All operations are
published. All changes are recorded.

3.2. The project's code is open (see [`LICENSE`](../../LICENSE)). The documents
are open under a free license (see [`LICENSE-DOCS`](../../LICENSE-DOCS)).

3.3. Records of decisions and operations are kept immutably (blockchain / IPFS /
public registries) to the extent technically possible at each stage.

## Article 4. Treasury Management

4.1. Funds exist on the blockchain and are managed through a Safe multisig,
smart contracts, DAO modules, and public registries.

4.2. Initial configuration: **5 guardians, 3 signatures out of 5**. The founder
has the same rights as the other guardians.

4.3. A temporary bootstrap wallet is used only to launch the infrastructure. The
bootstrap guardian is not an owner. Once the community is formed, the funds are
transferred to the public treasury.

4.4. **Real-funds latch.** Real funds enter the smart contracts only after: (a)
an independent security audit; (b) explicit, separate approval by the
operator/community; (c) the treasury being placed under a 3-of-5 Safe multisig
of living people. Until that moment — test networks and test funds only.

## Article 5. Fair Distribution

5.1. The distribution of aid follows the constitutional priority defined in
[`PRIORITIES.md`](PRIORITIES.md).

5.2. Aid must increase a person's self-reliance, not dependence.

5.3. Every person has dignity regardless of income, origin, education, age,
nationality, health condition, and social status.

## Article 6. Reward for Socially Beneficial Activity

6.1. The project may reward socially beneficial activity (helping people,
volunteering, developing the project, teaching, moderation, etc. — see
`MANIFESTO.md`).

6.2. **Reward never depends on recruiting new participants.**

## Article 7. Protection Against Abuse

7.1. The mechanisms from [`ANTI-ABUSE.md`](ANTI-ABUSE.md) apply: phased payouts,
limits, collective verification, public reports, audit, appeals, reputational
sanctions, temporary restrictions, exclusion of participants.

7.2. The system must make honesty more profitable than fraud.

## Article 8. Prohibitions

8.1. The prohibitions are listed in [`PRINCIPLES.md`](PRINCIPLES.md) (the
"Constitutional Prohibitions" section) and must be observed literally throughout
all code, all agents, and all public texts.

## Article 9. The Role of Artificial Intelligence

9.1. AI holds no power. AI owns no funds. AI serves the project's mission and
helps uphold the constitution.

9.2. AI agents (Guardian, Audit, Fairness, Reputation, Housing, Governance,
Mediator, Documentation) are service modules, not organs of authority.

## Article 10. Amending the Constitution

10.1. Amendments to the constitution are permitted only through a transparent
community governance procedure, publicly and with an explanation of the reasons.

10.2. No amendment may introduce a sole owner, abolish openness, introduce a
promise of returns, or otherwise violate the immutable core: Articles 1, 2, 3
and clause 6.2.
