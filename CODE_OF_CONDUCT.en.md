# Code of Conduct — Public Trust DAO

Public Trust DAO is a **public good, NOT an investment**. The project is built on
human dignity and mutual aid (see [`docs/en/PRINCIPLES.md`](docs/en/PRINCIPLES.md)).
This code describes how we communicate and collaborate so the space stays safe,
honest, and verifiable for everyone — especially the people in need for whom all
of this exists.

This is the canonical file (GitHub surfaces it on the "Community" tab). A short
version lives in [`CONTRIBUTING.en.md`](CONTRIBUTING.en.md); the full text is here.

---

## Our pledge

In the interest of an open and welcoming community, we — contributors, guardians,
and maintainers — pledge to make participation in the project a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, citizenship, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

**Behavior that builds a healthy environment:**

- respect for people; criticize ideas and code, not individuals;
- honesty and verifiability — claims are backed by links and facts;
- kindness to newcomers and patience with questions;
- accepting constructive criticism gracefully;
- keeping focus on the common good and on the dignity of the people being helped.

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- insults, demeaning comments, personal or political attacks, harassment in any
  form (public or private);
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome attention;
- publishing others' private information (address, correspondence, etc.) without
  explicit permission — **and never the data of people the fund is helping**;
- using the project for self-promotion, spam, pressure by urgency, or promises
  of profit;
- turning people in need into "content" or a PR opportunity;
- other conduct inappropriate in a professional mutual-aid community.

## Constitutional rails as part of conduct

This project is about the **safety** of a shared wallet, not power over it. So a
**breach of the safety rails is a breach of conduct**, not a "technical dispute":

- attempting to hide an operation or run it non-transparently;
- attempting to slip real funds, a mainnet deployment, or private keys past the
  rails (the project is **TESTNET-first**; real money only after an independent
  audit, a community decision, and a 3-of-5 Safe multisig of real people);
- attempting to bypass "one person = one vote" voting or to concentrate power
  over the treasury (see [`docs/en/GOVERNANCE.md`](docs/en/GOVERNANCE.md));
- promising returns, building a pyramid, paying for recruiting people — the
  literal prohibitions of [`docs/en/PRINCIPLES.md`](docs/en/PRINCIPLES.md).

A reminder: **contribution ≠ power over money.** No matter how much someone does,
it grants no right to direct funds — authorship is recognition, not ownership
(see [`AUTHORS.en.md`](AUTHORS.en.md)).

## Scope

This code applies in all project spaces — the repository (issues, pull requests,
discussions, code and documents), governance venues (e.g. off-chain voting), and
any channel where a person represents the project in public.

## Reporting

You can report unacceptable behavior or a breach of the rails:

- **Non-sensitive** (rudeness in a thread, a dispute about tone) — open an issue
  or flag it in the discussion/PR. Most matters are resolved openly and calmly.
- **Sensitive** (harassment, leak of personal data, an attempt to bypass the
  safety rails) — a private channel. A permanent `security@`/`conduct@` address
  will be published once the project email is set up (see
  [`docs/en/EMAIL-SETUP.md`](docs/en/EMAIL-SETUP.md)); until then, escalation is
  received by the project's human operators/guardians. Never post someone's
  personal data or secrets in an open issue.

All reports are handled in good faith and without publicizing the reporter's
identity. Decisions are made by people (guardians/maintainers), not by an
automated agent: the project's AI agents only **flag** problems — they decide
nothing and control nothing (Constitution, Art. 9).

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards and will take fair
corrective action proportionate to the violation: from a private warning and a
request to correct behavior, up to a temporary or permanent restriction of
participation in project spaces for serious or repeated violations. Actions are
transparent in substance (without revealing the reporter's identity) and may be
appealed — the appeals path is described in
[`docs/en/ANTI-ABUSE.md`](docs/en/ANTI-ABUSE.md).
